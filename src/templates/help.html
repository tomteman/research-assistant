Sifting through the results provided by Google Scholar can be a bit overwhelming. Research Assistant helps you keep track of important articles by labeling them. Simply click the “Add Label” button underneath the article to add it to your collection, which you can then access on-line from any computer. Want to view all articles belonging to a specific label? Simply click that label name on the left side of the screen.





Want to keep tabs on new articles, authors or publications? Let us do the dirty work for you. Creating a “Follow” will allow you to receive updates via email about new articles – tailored to your needs. Simply specify the parameters of the periodical search you wish to run, and we'll let you know if anything new comes up. For instance, you can add a Follow which searches for new articles citing a specific article (a great way to know if someone cited your article) – simply find that article, click the “Add Follow” button and make sure that the “Citing” box is checked (you can check additional boxes, but that will narrow down your results significantly). Obviously, you can specify other parameters for your Follow.






Sharing is caring. Found an interesting article? Want to start working together with colleagues? You can easily share a label with other people. Once they accept your invitation the shared label will become completely “communal” - any new articles you mark with this label will show up on their profile, and vice versa.





Keep even better track of your research by adding comments to your labeled articles. And remember – any comments you add to a shared label are visible to all members of that label, making collaboration even easier.





Research Assistant helps you find interesting articles in your field of research. We compare your activity on our site with that of other researchers. Using this comparison, we are able to recommend other articles that might interest you. These recommended items will then appear in the Suggestions area. You can then review these suggestions and remove an item using the No Thanks button; add it to your collection by labeling it; or create a Follow based on that article.
